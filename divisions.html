SADPS Divisions & Sub-Divisions

The San Andreas Department of Public Safety (SADPS) operates a number of specialized sub-divisions that enhance our operational capabilities, increase strategic flexibility, and ensure our department can respond effectively to a wide range of incidents and public safety needs.

While major departments such as Highway Patrol, Fire Rescue, and the Sheriff's Office play key roles in day-to-day enforcement and emergency services, it is the sub-divisions within SADPS that provide focused expertise, advanced tactics, and mission-specific resources. These teams are staffed by highly trained personnel and are tasked with assignments that often go beyond standard patrol responsibilities.

These sub-divisions allow SADPS to address high-risk scenarios, regulatory oversight, internal administration, and technological support, ensuring a well-rounded and professionally managed agency. Each unit plays a vital role in fulfilling our mission to protect life and property throughout the state.

Below is a breakdown of the current SADPS sub-divisions and their core functions:

SADPS Command Division

The Command Division is the executive branch of SADPS, responsible for overseeing policy, administration, department-wide coordination, and strategic leadership. Command staff manage interdepartmental operations, ensure compliance with statewide policies, and provide guidance to all SADPS personnel. This division sets the standard for professionalism, structure, and accountability across the agency.

Licensing & Regulatory Bureau

This bureau is tasked with managing licensing, compliance, and regulatory oversight within the state. Whether it’s issuing firearm permits, reviewing commercial driver qualifications, or overseeing safety certifications, the Licensing & Regulatory Bureau ensures that individuals and businesses operate within established laws and public safety guidelines.

Special Enforcement Unit (SEU)

The Special Enforcement Unit (SEU) is a tactical sub-division focused on high-risk, high-priority missions including gang suppression, warrant execution, narcotics operations, and armed stand-offs. SEU operators are among the most highly trained personnel in SADPS, prepared to resolve dangerous incidents that require advanced coordination and precision.

Public Safety Communications & Technology Division

Responsible for maintaining SADPS’s operational technology and communication systems, this division oversees CAD systems, radio infrastructure, records management, and internal IT services. Without this division, inter-agency communication, real-time tracking, and emergency coordination would be impossible. They are the behind-the-scenes backbone of our digital operations.

SADPS Patrol Division & Specialized Units

The SADPS Patrol Division is composed of troopers who serve in a statewide capacity, supporting enforcement efforts wherever needed. Within this division are specialized sub-units that address specific operational priorities, including:

D.A.R.T. – Dangerous Aggressive Reckless Traffic Unit - A proactive enforcement team focused on targeting dangerous, aggressive, and reckless driving behaviors. D.A.R.T. works state highways, known danger zones, and supports high-speed interventions to reduce fatalities and improve roadway safety.

Aviation/Air Support Unit - This airborne division supports ground operations with aerial surveillance, search and rescue, traffic monitoring, and tactical overwatch. The air unit enhances visibility and response times in critical situations, especially in remote or high-speed pursuits.

K9 Unit - The K9 Unit utilizes specially trained police dogs for tracking suspects, detecting narcotics, conducting searches, and supporting officer safety. K9 handlers and their partners provide valuable assistance during high-risk operations and are essential to SADPS’s tactical capability.

Field Training Unit (FTU) - FTU is responsible for the structured onboarding and training of new recruits. Experienced troopers provide hands-on mentorship and assessment to ensure all new personnel meet SADPS standards of conduct, performance, and safety.

Commercial Vehicle Enforcement (CVE) - This unit ensures the safety and compliance of commercial vehicles operating on state roadways. CVE officers conduct inspections, enforce weight restrictions, and monitor freight movement to reduce roadway hazards and maintain public safety.

Together, these sub-divisions allow SADPS to operate as a highly capable, mission-ready, and professionally managed agency. They represent the specialized strength behind our general patrol operations and ensure that SADPS remains adaptable, innovative, and effective in meeting the complex demands of modern public safety.
